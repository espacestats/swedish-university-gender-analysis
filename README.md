# swedish-university-gender-analysis
Power BI analysis of gender patterns in completed degrees across selected Swedish universities using SCB data.
# Swedish University Gender Analysis

This project explores the gender split in completed higher-education degrees across selected Swedish universities over the past 15 years.

## Project question
How has the gender balance in completed degrees changed across selected Swedish universities over time?

## Background
This analysis originally started as an attempt to explore a broader claim about whether AI is pushing students away from demanding higher education. While I did not find clear support for that in the completed-degree data I explored, the question led me to a different and more interesting angle: the long-term gender split in completed degrees at Swedish universities.

## Data source
- Statistics Sweden (SCB)

## Universities included
- Chalmers
- KTH
- SSE
- Lund University
- Uppsala University
- Stockholm University
- University of Gothenburg

## Tools used
- Power BI
- Excel

## Key findings
- Women completed more degrees than men across the universities in this dataset overall.
- On average, women completed about **1.37 times as many degrees as men**.
- Chalmers and KTH remained male-dominated throughout the full period.
- Women’s share increased slightly over time at the technical universities, but the overall pattern remained fairly stable.

## Output
The final visual compares selected academic years and highlights how gender patterns differ across universities.
